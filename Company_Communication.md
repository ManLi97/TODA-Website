# Company Communication.md

## Purpose
This file is a communication guide for generating **German mock copy** for the TODA website.

Use this as a **directional brand and tone guide**, not as a source for final polished copy.
The goal is to replace the current English website copy with **German placeholder copy** that already feels close to the intended brand voice.

Important:
- This document is written in English for implementation clarity.
- The **website copy itself must be in German**.
- Do **not** add CTA copy. The current website version should not rely on CTA sections or CTA buttons.

---

## Brand Core
TODA should feel like a focused, modern product built for the real day-to-day life of tattoo artists and studios.

It should not sound like generic SaaS.
It should not sound like startup marketing.
It should not sound overly polished, loud, or corporate.

At its core, TODA stands for:
- less chaos
- less manual admin
- less back and forth
- clearer workflows
- better overview
- more professionalism in daily operations
- more focus on the actual work

---

## Important Brand Context
Part of the TODA team consists of tattoo artists themselves.

This matters in the communication.
The copy should subtly reflect that TODA is not built by outsiders guessing industry problems, but by people who know the studio reality firsthand.

The tone should communicate:
- we understand the day-to-day reality
- we know where friction happens
- we are building from actual experience, not theory

Do not overstate this.
Keep it calm, confident, and embedded in the language.

---

## Positioning
TODA is a tool for tattoo artists / studios that brings structure, clarity, and professionalism into workflows that are otherwise often fragmented, manual, or messy.

The communication should suggest:
- TODA removes friction from everyday work
- TODA creates clarity without adding complexity
- TODA is practical, not theoretical
- TODA helps studios work in a more structured and professional way
- TODA is built for this industry specifically

---

## Main Pain Points To Reflect
The copy should be written around real operational tension points such as:
- too many manual steps
- too much back and forth
- scattered communication
- missing overview
- admin work taking too much time and energy
- workflows that feel improvised instead of structured
- software that does not really fit the tattoo business

Important:
Do not sound dramatic or complain too much.
Name the problems clearly, but in a composed and mature way.

It should **not** feel:
- playful

- corporate

---

## Language Style
Prefer language that feels grounded and useful.

Good direction:
- bringt Ordnung in den Alltag
- schafft mehr Übersicht
- reduziert unnötiges Hin und Her
- macht Abläufe klarer
- entlastet im Studioalltag
- hilft, professioneller zu arbeiten
- ist für den realen Betrieb gemacht

Avoid language like:
- revolutionär
- Gamechanger
- Next Generation
- maximale Effizienzsteigerung
- innovatives Ökosystem
- cutting-edge
- all-in-one Plattform

TODA should sound like a product that understands operations, not like a pitch deck.

---

## Writing Rules For German Mock Copy
Use these rules when rewriting English website copy into German:
- keep sentences short to medium length
- prefer clarity over cleverness
- use few adjectives
- use real-world, practical wording
- make the value tangible
- sound precise, not loud
- avoid buzzwords
- avoid exaggerated claims
- avoid generic SaaS phrases
- avoid heavy marketing language

The copy should feel like:
“Finally something that brings order into the studio day-to-day.”
Not like:
“A revolutionary platform transforming the tattoo industry.”

---

## Content Priority
When writing sections, prioritize **effect before feature**.

Start with:
- what becomes easier
- what becomes clearer
- what disappears
- what feels more structured
- what helps in the daily reality

Only after that, support the message with product logic.

So the order should usually be:
1. friction / pain
2. practical value
3. structured product benefit

Not:
1. feature dump
2. abstract claims
3. generic value statement

## Direction For Website Copy
This is **not final copy**, only directional guidance for the mock content.

### Hero Direction
The hero should communicate that TODA helps tattoo artists and studios bring more structure, clarity, and professionalism into their daily work.

### Supporting Message Direction
The supporting message should revolve around:
- less chaos
- less admin overhead
- less back and forth
- more overview
- clearer workflows
- a calmer and more professional studio operation

### Feature Section Direction
Feature-related copy should not read like a technical product sheet.
It should connect each feature area to a real daily problem and a real operational benefit.

### Brand Trust Direction
Where helpful, the copy may subtly communicate that part of the team knows the tattoo industry from the inside, because tattoo artists are part of the team.

Do this in a restrained way.
No chest-beating.
No overexplaining.

---

## Practical Instruction For Implementation
When replacing existing English website text:
- rewrite all visible website copy into German mock copy
- keep the structure lean
- keep section meaning intact
- adapt wording to this communication guide
- do not force literal translation
- prefer natural German over direct English structure
- do not invent loud sales copy
- do not add CTA-heavy language
- do not add new marketing sections just to fill space

The result should already feel like TODA, even though it is still only mock copy.
